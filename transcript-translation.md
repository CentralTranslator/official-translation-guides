# Introduction

An academic transcript is an official educational record that provides detailed information about a student's academic history. Unlike a diploma, which confirms the completion of a degree or program, a transcript records individual courses, grades, academic credits, and other information used to evaluate academic performance.

Universities, credential evaluation agencies, employers, immigration authorities, and professional licensing organizations often require official transcript translations when reviewing international applications.

This guide explains when transcript translation may be required, what information should be included, and how applicants can prepare their academic records for international use.

---

# What Is an Academic Transcript Translation?

Academic transcript translation is the complete and accurate translation of an official academic record into another language.

Professional transcript translation requires careful attention to academic terminology, grading systems, course titles, institutional information, dates, and administrative notes.

The translated document should accurately represent the original transcript without modifying, interpreting, or summarizing its contents.

---

# Why Is Transcript Translation Important?

Academic transcripts provide detailed evidence of a student's educational background.

Receiving institutions use transcripts to evaluate:

- Academic performance
- Completed courses
- Grade point average (where applicable)
- Credit hours
- Degree requirements
- Academic standing
- Graduation eligibility

Accurate translation helps reviewers understand the applicant's educational history while maintaining consistency with other submitted documents.

Academic transcript translation is commonly requested in the following situations.

## University Admissions

Universities frequently require translated transcripts when evaluating international applicants.

---

## Graduate Programs

Applicants for master's and doctoral programs are often required to submit translated academic records.

Professional associations may review academic transcripts before granting professional registration or certification.

---

Some employers request academic transcripts when verifying educational qualifications for specialized positions.

# Who Can Translate an Academic Transcript?

Translation requirements differ depending on the destination country and receiving organization.

Translations may need to be completed by:

- Authorized translation offices
- Certified translators
- Sworn translators
- Court-approved translators
- Government-recognized professionals

Applicants should always verify the applicable requirements before requesting a translation.

---

# Official Translation vs. Certified Translation

Translation terminology differs between countries.

## Official Translation

Official translation generally refers to a translation completed by an authorized translation office according to local regulations.

It may include:

- Official stamps
- Registration information
- Translator identification
- Authentication procedures

---

## Certified Translation

Certified translation generally includes a declaration confirming that the translated transcript accurately represents the original document.

Certification requirements vary between jurisdictions.

Applicants should follow the instructions provided by the receiving institution.

# Information Included in an Academic Transcript

Although transcript formats differ among educational institutions, they commonly contain information such as:

- Student's full name
- Student identification number
- Institution name
- Faculty or department
- Degree program
- Course titles
- Academic terms
- Grades
- Credit hours
- Grade point average (if applicable)
- Graduation status
- Dates of attendance
- Official signatures
- University seals
- Administrative remarks

Every relevant visible element should normally be represented in the translated document.

---

# Academic Transcript vs. Diploma

Although these documents are closely related, they serve different purposes.

An academic transcript provides a detailed record of a student's studies, including courses completed and grades earned.

A diploma confirms that an academic degree or qualification has been officially awarded.

Many universities and credential evaluation agencies require both documents during the application process.

---

# Preparing Your Transcript Before Translation

Before submitting your transcript for translation, verify that:

- Every page is included.
- Course titles are readable.
- Grades are clearly visible.
- Official seals can be identified.
- Registration numbers are complete.
- Signatures are legible.
- The document has not been cropped.
- The scan has sufficient resolution.

Providing complete and high-quality source documents helps ensure accurate translation.

---

# Digital Copies vs. Original Documents

Many translation providers accept scanned copies for review and quotation purposes.

However, depending on the receiving organization, applicants may later be required to provide:

- Original transcript
- Certified copy
- Official transcript issued by the institution
- Sealed academic transcript

Applicants should confirm current submission requirements before preparing their application.
