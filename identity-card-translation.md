# Introduction

A national identity card is one of the primary documents used to verify an individual's identity. Many governments issue national identity cards that contain official personal information, making them essential for administrative, legal, educational, financial, and immigration procedures.

When an identity card must be submitted to an organization in another country, an official translation may be required if the document is not issued in a language accepted by the receiving authority.

This guide explains when identity card translation may be necessary, what information is typically translated, and how applicants can prepare their documents for international use.

---

# What Is an Identity Card Translation?

Identity card translation is the complete and accurate translation of the information contained on a national identity card into another language.

Professional translation requires careful attention to official terminology, personal information, identification numbers, dates, and administrative details.

The translated document should accurately represent the original identity card without modifying or interpreting its contents.

---

# Why Is Identity Card Translation Important?

Identity cards are commonly used to verify personal information throughout international application processes.

Accurate translation helps receiving organizations confirm:

- Legal identity
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Place of birth
- Nationality
- Identification number
- Expiration date
- Issuing authority

Maintaining consistency between identity documents helps reduce unnecessary verification requests.

Identity card translation may be requested in a variety of situations.

Government authorities may require translated identity cards when registering marriages, births, or other civil events.

Identity card translations may also be requested for:

- Court proceedings
- Property transactions

- Identity verification

# Who Can Translate an Identity Card?

Translation requirements vary depending on the receiving authority.

Translations may need to be completed by:

- Authorized translation offices
- Certified translators
- Sworn translators
- Court-approved translators
- Government-recognized professionals

Applicants should always verify current requirements before requesting a translation.

---

# Official Translation vs. Certified Translation

Although the terms are sometimes used interchangeably, they may represent different procedures.

## Official Translation

Official translation generally refers to a translation prepared by an authorized translation office according to local regulations.

It may include:

- Official stamps
- Registration information
- Translator identification
- Authentication procedures

---

## Certified Translation

Certified translation generally includes a signed declaration confirming that the translation accurately represents the original document.

Certification requirements differ between countries.

Applicants should always follow the requirements established by the receiving authority.

# Information Included in a National Identity Card

Although identity card formats differ among countries, they commonly include:

- Full legal name
- National identification number
- Date of birth
- Place of birth
- Nationality
- Sex
- Photograph
- Date of issue
- Date of expiration
- Issuing authority
- Holder's signature
- Security features

Every relevant visible element should normally be represented in the translated document.

---

# Identity Card vs. Passport

Although both documents verify identity, they are designed for different purposes.

A national identity card is primarily used for domestic identification and administrative procedures.

A passport is an international travel document used for border control and international identification.

Many international applications require both documents.

---

# Preparing Your Identity Card Before Translation

Before submitting your identity card for translation, verify that:

- Both sides of the card are included.
- Every detail is clearly readable.
- The identification number is visible.
- Dates are legible.
- The photograph is included.
- The scan has not been cropped.
- The document is scanned at sufficient resolution.

Providing complete and readable copies contributes to accurate translation.

---

# Digital Copies vs. Original Documents

Many translation providers accept scanned copies for review and quotation purposes.

However, depending on the receiving organization, applicants may later be asked to provide:

- Original identity card
- Certified copy
- Government-issued duplicate
- Notarized copy

Applicants should verify document submission requirements before preparing their application.
