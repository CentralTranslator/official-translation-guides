# Introduction

A diploma is an official academic document confirming that a student has successfully completed a program of study and has been awarded a degree, certificate, or qualification by an educational institution.

When applying to universities, immigration authorities, employers, or professional licensing organizations in another country, applicants may be required to submit an official translation of their diploma.

This guide explains when diploma translation is required, what information is typically translated, and how applicants can prepare their documents for international use.

---

# What Is a Diploma Translation?

Diploma translation is the complete and accurate translation of an academic diploma into another language.

Unlike general translation, diploma translation requires careful attention to academic terminology, institutional information, degree titles, dates, signatures, and official seals.

The translated document should accurately reflect every relevant element appearing on the original diploma without altering its meaning.

---

# Why Is Diploma Translation Important?

Educational documents are frequently reviewed to verify an applicant's academic qualifications.

Accurate translation helps receiving institutions confirm:

- Academic qualification
- Degree title
- Field of study
- Awarding institution
- Graduation date
- Academic level
- Official recognition

Accurate translations also help maintain consistency across other academic documents submitted with an application.

Diploma translation is commonly requested in the following situations.

## University Admissions

Universities often require translated diplomas when evaluating international applicants.

## Immigration Applications

---

Professional organizations may request translated diplomas when evaluating applicants seeking licenses or certifications.

---

## Employment Abroad

International employers frequently request translated educational qualifications during recruitment.

---

## Credential Evaluation

Educational evaluation organizations may require translated diplomas when assessing foreign qualifications.

---

## Scholarship Applications

Applicants seeking scholarships may need to provide officially translated educational documents together with other supporting records.

# Who Can Translate a Diploma?

Translation requirements differ between countries and organizations.

Depending on local regulations, translations may need to be completed by:

- Authorized translation offices
- Certified translators
- Sworn translators
- Court-approved translators
- Government-recognized professionals

Applicants should always verify current requirements before requesting a translation.

---

# Official Translation vs. Certified Translation

Although these terms are often used together, they may have different meanings depending on local regulations.

## Official Translation

Official translation generally refers to a translation completed by an authorized translation office in accordance with applicable regulations.

It may include:

- Official stamps
- Registration information
- Translator identification
- Authentication procedures

---

## Certified Translation

Certified translation generally includes a declaration confirming that the translated document accurately represents the original diploma.

Certification requirements vary by jurisdiction.

Applicants should verify the requirements established by the receiving organization.

---

# Information Included in a Diploma

Although diploma formats vary among educational institutions, most diplomas contain information such as:

- Student's full name
- Degree title
- Field of study
- Academic level
- Educational institution
- Graduation date
- Date of issue
- Registration number
- Official signatures
- University seals
- Institutional logos

Every relevant visible element should normally be represented in the translated document.

---

# Diploma vs. Academic Transcript

A diploma and an academic transcript are different documents.

A diploma confirms that a degree has been awarded.

An academic transcript provides detailed information about courses completed, grades received, academic credits, and overall academic performance.

Many universities request both documents during the admission process.

---

# Preparing Your Diploma Before Translation

Before submitting your diploma for translation, verify that:

- Every page is included.
- The document is clearly readable.
- Official seals are visible.
- Signatures are legible.
- Registration numbers are complete.
- No part of the document has been cropped.
- The scan has sufficient resolution.

Clear source documents contribute to more accurate translations.

---

# Digital Copies vs. Original Documents

Many translation providers accept scanned copies for review and quotation purposes.

However, depending on the receiving organization, applicants may later be asked to provide:

- Original diploma
- Certified copy
- Notarized copy
- Official duplicate issued by the educational institution

Applicants should verify document submission requirements before preparing their application.

---

# Frequently Requested Supporting Documents

Diploma translation is often submitted together with:

- Academic transcript
- Passport
- National identity card
- Birth certificate
- Graduation certificate
- Enrollment confirmation
- Professional certificates
- Credential evaluation documents

Providing related academic documents together helps maintain consistency throughout the application.
