# Introduction

When documents are intended for use outside the country where they were issued, foreign authorities often require proof that the documents are authentic.

One of the most widely recognized methods of document authentication is the Apostille Certificate.

An apostille confirms the authenticity of the signature, seal, or stamp appearing on a public document. It simplifies international document recognition between countries that are members of the Hague Apostille Convention.

Understanding whether your destination country accepts apostilles or requires a different legalization process is essential before preparing your documents.

---

# What Is an Apostille?

An apostille is an official certificate issued by a competent government authority that verifies the authenticity of a public document.

It does not certify that the document's contents are correct.

Instead, it confirms that:

- The signature is genuine.
- The official signing the document had the authority to do so.
- The seal or stamp is authentic.

The apostille certificate allows public documents to be accepted in other participating countries without additional embassy legalization.

---

# Why Is an Apostille Required?

Many government agencies, universities, employers, and legal institutions require authenticated documents before accepting them.

An apostille provides confidence that the submitted document is officially recognized by the issuing country.

Common situations include:

- Immigration
- Student visa applications
- University admissions
- Employment abroad
- International marriage registration
- Business registration
- International contracts
- Court proceedings
- Adoption
- Citizenship applications

---

# Apostille vs. Legalization

Although these terms are often confused, they refer to different authentication procedures.

## Apostille

An apostille is used when both the issuing country and the destination country are members of the Hague Apostille Convention.

The authentication process is generally simplified because participating countries recognize apostilles issued by one another.

---

## Legalization

Legalization is generally required when the destination country does not participate in the Hague Apostille Convention.

The process often involves multiple authorities, such as:

- Government ministries
- Ministry of Foreign Affairs
- Embassy or Consulate
- Other competent authorities

The exact procedure varies depending on the destination country.

---

# Which Documents Can Receive an Apostille?

Depending on national regulations, apostilles may be issued for many public documents, including:

- Birth certificates
- Marriage certificates
- Death certificates
- Divorce certificates
- Academic diplomas
- University transcripts
- Court documents
- Powers of attorney
- Company registration documents
- Commercial certificates
- Notarized documents
- Criminal record certificates

Not every document qualifies automatically. Local regulations determine eligibility.

# Countries That Accept Apostilles

More than one hundred countries recognize apostilles under the Hague Apostille Convention.

Examples include:

- Germany
- France
- Italy
- Spain
- Netherlands
- Australia
- Japan
- South Korea
- United Kingdom
- United States (for participating public documents under applicable rules)

Applicants should always verify the latest requirements with the receiving authority because document policies may change.

---

# Countries That Do Not Accept Apostilles

If the destination country is not a member of the Hague Apostille Convention, documents generally require legalization instead of an apostille.

Requirements vary depending on the destination country.

Applicants should consult the relevant embassy or government authority before beginning the authentication process.

---

# How the Apostille Process Works

Although procedures differ among countries, the process generally follows these steps:

1. Obtain the original document.
2. Complete any required official translation.
3. Obtain notarization if required.
4. Submit the document to the competent apostille authority.
5. Receive the apostille certificate.
6. Submit the authenticated document to the receiving organization.

Some countries require translation before apostille, while others require translation afterward.

Always verify the correct sequence for your destination.
